Image Sets
Crime Evidence
Probe Images
Probe stimuli are crime-specific images that only a guilty subject would recognise — victim's face, weapon used, stolen item, crime scene detail. These are the critical stimuli that will elicit a P300 response in a subject with concealed knowledge.

Distractors
Neutral Images
Neutral stimuli are unrelated, everyday images with no connection to the crime or the suspect. They serve as the baseline — an innocent subject will respond identically to these and to probe images. Minimum 3× the number of probe images required.

Timing Parameters (ms)
Each stimulus cycle = fixation (500ms) + image (400ms) + blank interval (1200ms) = 2.1 seconds per trial. These values follow published CIT/P300 protocols. P300 peaks 300–800ms post-stimulus — ISI must exceed 1000ms for clean epoch separation.
